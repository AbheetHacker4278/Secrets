
Grand Theft Auto V (GTA V)
Synopsis:
Grand Theft Auto V (GTA V) is an open-world action-adventure video game developed by Rockstar North and published by Rockstar Games. The game follows three protagonists - Michael De Santa, Franklin Clinton, and Trevor Philips - as they commit various heists and criminal activities in the fictional city of Los Santos and its surrounding areas.
Gameplay:
GTA V features a vast open world that players can freely explore on foot, by vehicle, or by aircraft. The game's missions involve a diverse range of activities, including robberies, assassinations, street races, and more.
Characters:

Michael De Santa: A retired bank robber who is trying to adjust to a new life with his wife and children.
Franklin Clinton: A young street hustler who is looking to make a name for himself.
Trevor Philips: A violent and unpredictable meth dealer who is Michael's former partner in crime.

Setting:
GTA V is set in the fictional city of Los Santos, which is based on Los Angeles, California. The game also features a number of surrounding areas, including the countryside, deserts, and mountains.
Features:

Open-world environment: Players can explore a massive and detailed open world on foot, by vehicle, or by aircraft.
Diverse missions: The game features a wide variety of missions, including robberies, assassinations, street races, and more.
Multiple protagonists: Players can switch between playing as Michael, Franklin, or Trevor, each with their own unique abilities and perspectives.
Detailed graphics: GTA V boasts cutting-edge graphics that bring Los Santos to life with stunning detail.
Online multiplayer: The game offers an online multiplayer mode called GTA Online, where players can team up or compete with others in a variety of game modes.

Reception:
GTA V was a critical and commercial success, receiving widespread praise for its open-world gameplay, compelling characters, and detailed graphics. It became the best-selling video game of all time at the time of its release and has since sold over 170 million copies worldwide.
